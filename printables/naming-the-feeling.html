Naming the Feeling

Most people know "I feel bad." This worksheet helps you get specific — because the more precisely you name an emotion, the less power it has to run in the background. Getting from "upset" to "humiliated" or "disappointed" is meaningful progress.

How to use this

Start with what happened. Then scan the vocabulary grid — not to find the "right" word, but to find the most accurate one. You can have feelings from multiple clusters at the same time. That's not confusion, that's how emotions actually work.

Feelings carry needs. Anger often needs acknowledgment. Sadness needs comfort. Fear needs safety or information. What might this feeling be asking for?

Closing reflection

How did it feel to name this more precisely? Did anything shift — even slightly?
