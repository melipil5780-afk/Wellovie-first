Before Your First Session · Part 3 of 3

What I Want You to Know

This worksheet is about the therapeutic relationship itself — how you work, what's helped before, what you're hoping for, and what you're nervous about. Good therapy is built on this kind of honesty. The more your therapist understands about how you show up, the faster you can do real work together.

Why this worksheet matters

Most of what makes therapy work happens in the relationship between you and your therapist. This section helps your therapist understand how to work with you specifically — not just with the problem you're bringing.

1

Previous experience with therapy or support

Have you been in therapy before, worked with a counselor, or sought formal support? Tell us about it honestly — especially what helped, what didn't, and why you're here again or for the first time.

If you've had therapy before — what worked? What didn't? Why did you stop?

How you show up when things get hard

In difficult conversations or when you feel vulnerable, how do you tend to respond? Check anything that feels true — even if you're not proud of it. This helps your therapist meet you where you actually are.

Anything else about how you tend to respond when things get difficult?

4

What you're nervous about

Starting therapy takes courage — and most people bring some fear with them. What are you nervous about, worried about, or hoping won't happen?

Any fears not listed, or anything you want to say more about?

5

How you prefer to be supported

People need different things from a therapist. There's no right answer — this is about what actually works for you.

6

Your goals for therapy

What would tell you that therapy was worthwhile? What do you want to be different — in how you feel, how you function, or who you are — when this work has done something?

7

What you're not ready for

This is important. Is there anything you're not ready to go near yet? Naming it now means your therapist can respect it.
