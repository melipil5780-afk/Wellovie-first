The moment that matters

What your client feels
before they ever contact you.

“They’re not reading your bio for your credentials. They’re reading it to feel safe. That’s a completely different thing to write for.”

A potential client searching for a therapist is in one of the most vulnerable moments of their life. They’ve made the decision to ask for help — which is enormous. Now they’re sitting with a browser tab open, reading your site, trying to answer one question: will I feel seen by this person?

Most therapy websites are designed by people who have never been in that position professionally. They prioritize credentials, certifications, and clinical language. What they miss is the emotional register of that moment entirely.

We know that moment from both sides. And every word on your site is written for it.
