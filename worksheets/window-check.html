The Window Check

Regulation isn't about staying calm — it's about staying you. This worksheet helps you understand your own window of tolerance, recognize when you've left it, and build a personal map of what brings you back.

Date
Session / Solo
Before you begin

This worksheet works best after a moment of dysregulation has passed — not in the middle of one. If you're currently overwhelmed, set this aside and return when you have some ground under you. Your answers auto-save to this browser.

1 Your window of tolerance

The window of tolerance is the zone where you can feel emotions without being overwhelmed by them — where you can think, respond, and stay present. Below are the three zones. Read each one and add what your personal version looks like.

Hyperarousal — too much activation
Anxiety, panic, rage, racing thoughts, can't slow down. The nervous system is flooded. Hard to think clearly or feel safe.

Window of tolerance — regulated
Emotions are present but not overwhelming. You can think and feel at the same time. You can stay in the room with hard things.

Closing note
Regulation isn't a destination — it's a practice of returning. You will leave your window. The work is learning to notice earlier, and to come back with less judgment of yourself for having left in the first place. Presence, not performance.
